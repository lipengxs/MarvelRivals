Marvel Rivals Team Composition Guide

Good lineups are not a list of “best” heroes. They are a shared plan for pressure, protection, objective control, and a clean reset when the first plan fails.

1. Start With One Win Condition

Before selecting heroes, say what the team is trying to accomplish. A composition can prioritize holding a space, forcing a fast pick, surviving an extended objective, or denying a dangerous lane. One clear answer prevents six players from taking unrelated fights.

  • Write the first objective plan in one sentence.
  • Choose where the team wants to fight, not only whom it wants to eliminate.
  • Keep a reset route in mind before committing resources.

2. Give Every Player a Job

Use roles as a starting point, then identify actual jobs: opening pressure, protecting a vulnerable teammate, contesting space, confirming a target, and calling a retreat. If two heroes perform the same job, name the missing job before the match starts.

  • Who begins the engagement?
  • Who can protect the back line or objective?
  • Who confirms a low-health target?
  • Who calls the reset when the fight is lost?

3. Plan the First Fight

Use a simple three-call routine: entry point, first pressure target, and reset signal. This is more useful than changing heroes repeatedly after a single loss. A lineup improves when players repeat a clear plan long enough to understand where it breaks.

  • Enter together rather than trading isolated duels.
  • Do not chase beyond the objective without a reason.
  • Change one weakness after a review, not the entire composition.

4. Review Synergy Without Guesswork

Track what actually happened: which hero opened space, which target escaped, where the team lost line of sight, and whether the reset was late. Link this review to hero pages and official profile updates instead of treating a static tier list as live data.
