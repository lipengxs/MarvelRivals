Marvel Rivals Hero Roles Guide

Choose a hero around a team job, not a permanent ranking. The useful question is whether the lineup can create pressure, protect a vulnerable player, contest an objective, and reset cleanly.

Duelist: Convert Pressure

Use a Duelist when the team needs to turn an opening into a confirmed advantage. The job is not simply damage: it is choosing a safe angle, synchronizing with the team’s entry, and leaving when the fight is already won or lost.

  • Ask who creates the opening.
  • Do not chase beyond the objective without a plan.
  • Call the target or route before committing.

Vanguard: Shape the Fight

A Vanguard can help decide where the team is allowed to stand. Focus on space, sightlines, protection, and the moment to disengage. A strong hold is often more valuable than forcing an unsupported elimination.

  • Identify the space worth holding.
  • Leave a route for allies to reset.
  • Communicate before moving the frontline.

Strategist: Sustain the Plan

A Strategist’s value is tied to timing and information. Support a clear objective, preserve resources for the next important moment, and communicate when the team should slow down instead of taking an isolated duel.

  • Keep sightlines intentional.
  • Signal resource windows and danger.
  • Help the team leave a lost fight together.

Use Profiles, Not Assumptions

Hero rosters and role details change. Start with the current profile, then add your own labelled notes from matches. Avoid publishing a named “best counter” or ranking without a version, source, and test context.
